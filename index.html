Play together. Keep it fair.

Survival multiplayer with a player-run economy and a few conveniences—shop, auctions, homes—on a world we fund together. The core loop stays close to vanilla: we’re not chasing a modpack or minigame-server vibe.

Endcity Survival is a community server: hosting is paid month to month with help from players who want the server to stay up. There’s no pay-to-win—donations cover the bill, not perks.

Fair play matters—see Rules for how we handle cheats, mods, and community conduct. Everything else is normal survival: build, trade, explore.

How we run the server

Hosting costs land around $50 per month. Donations and the goal bar in the sidebar go straight toward that—not ranks, crates, or gameplay boosts. Surplus when we’re ahead carries to the next bill so the math stays transparent.

Vanilla-first. Mining, building, and exploring stay the point. Plugins cover economy, crossplay, and quality-of-life (homes, warps, messaging)—enough to trade and travel together, not enough to replace the survival game underneath.

Rules

Three principles—staff apply them with context, not robot bans.

  1. No hacking or cheating — including x-ray, fly hacks, auto-clickers meant to bypass human play, or anything that mimics unfair client abilities.
  2. No unfair gameplay advantages — if it gives you an edge others can’t reasonably match, ask staff before using it.
  3. Respect the community — griefing or toxicity can still get you removed even if it isn’t “technical cheating.”

Mods & clients: Quality-of-life (e.g. zoom) is usually fine. Anything that reveals hidden ores, entities, or automates combat/building beyond fair human play is not. See the prohibited mods list for Java-only mods we ban to keep Bedrock players on fair ground.

Enforcement: Staff may warn, mute, kick, or ban depending on severity and history. If you’re unsure whether something is allowed, ask before you rely on it in a competitive situation.

Economy

Money is earned in-world—farming, selling, player trade—and spent at the server shop or with each other. Nothing here is bought with real money.

Balances & trade

Pay other players, check your balance, see who’s richest, and choose whether you accept incoming payments—handy when you want privacy from tips or spam.

Shop & selling

Buy from the server shop at listed prices, or turn loot into balance through the sell GUI and bulk-sell options so you’re not babysitting chests.

Auction house

List what you’re holding for a price, search what others listed, and track active sales, recent sales, and expired listings from one place.

Team systems and bounties are on the roadmap—watch Discord for when they go live.

Community

Hang out on Discord for patch notes, build screenshots, and voice. In-game help and the rules screen are there when you need a quick reminder.
